ONE PIECE — A Legendary Journey

ONE PIECE (stylized in all caps) is a legendary Japanese manga series created by Eiichiro Oda. The story follows Monkey D. Luffy and his crew, the Straw Hat Pirates, as they journey across the Grand Line in pursuit of the mythical treasure known as the "One Piece" — a prize that promises the title of the King of the Pirates.

First serialized in Weekly Shōnen Jump in July 1997, it has been compiled into 111 volumes as of March 2025. The series has been licensed for English release by Viz Media in North America and the UK, and by Madman Entertainment in Australia.

The franchise has expanded to include an anime adaptation by Toei Animation (airing since 1999), a festival film by Production I.G, 14 animated feature films, a live-action Netflix series (released in 2023), and a wide range of merchandise, trading cards, and video games.

Praised for its storytelling, world-building, humor, and art, ONE PIECE has earned numerous awards and global acclaim. As of August 2022, it has sold over 516.6 million copies in 61 countries — making it the best-selling manga and best-selling comic book series in history.

The series has broken multiple publishing records:

  • Guinness World Record for "most copies published for the same comic book series by a single author" (2015 & 2022)
  • Top manga for 11 consecutive years (2008–2018)
  • Only manga with 1M+ copies sold in every volume (100+ volumes)
  • Highest initial print runs in Japan

ONE PIECE isn't just a story — it's a legacy.
